Welcome!


Hi, my name is Khanh N. Dang. I am an Assistant Professor of VNU University of Engineering and Technology, Vietnam National University, Hanoi (VNU), Vietnam. I also work as a Researcher at the VNU-key Laboratory of Smart Integrated Systems. I am currently on a sabbatical leave and working as a Visting Researcher at the Adaptive System Lab of the University of Aizu, Japan.

I have worked on a wide range of topics. I started my career by doing my internship in ASIC design and completed my bachelor thesis with an FPGA project. I later worked on a full-hardware video compression processor using H.264/AVC standards. I completed my Ph.D. thesis with fault-tolerance methods for 3D-ICs and 3D-NoCs. I also spent time working on error correction codes and the enhancement algorithms for them. I have successfully filed several Japanese patents (one pending) on using error correction codes for detecting and localizing multiple defects on wires. Recently, I have been working on thermal aware designs and neuromorphic systems. I and my colleagues also found our interest in applying machine learning techniques to system design.

Please find more details about me in the profile, research, and publication pages.
